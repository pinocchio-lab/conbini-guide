🏪 How to Use Japanese Convenience Stores

Japanese convenience stores are safe, clean, and easy to use for tourists.

🔥 Heating Food

Staff can heat your food at the cashier.

💳 Payment

Most stores accept cash, credit cards, and IC cards.

🗑 Trash Bins

Trash bins are usually near the exit or outside the store.

⚠️ Public trash bins are rare in Japan, so convenience stores are very useful.

🚻 Toilets

Some convenience stores have free toilets for customers.

💡 Toilets are usually clean and very helpful during long trips.

🌸 Seasonal Products

Japanese convenience stores often sell seasonal snacks and drinks.

💡 Limited-time products are very popular in Japan.

🚉 IC Card Recharge

Some convenience stores allow you to recharge transportation IC cards like Suica or PASMO.

💡 This is useful when traveling by train in Japan.

🤫 Quiet Atmosphere

Japanese convenience stores are usually quiet and calm.

💡 Speaking loudly on the phone inside the store is uncommon in Japan.

🧻 Wet Towels

Convenience stores often provide free wet towels with food purchases.

💡 Wet towels are useful before eating during travel.

🏧 ATM

7-Eleven ATMs often support international cards.

💡 7-Eleven ATMs are one of the easiest ways for tourists to get Japanese yen.

🍽 Eat-in Space

Some convenience stores have small eat-in areas where you can sit and eat.

💡 Eat-in spaces are useful on rainy days or during long walks.

📶 Free Wi-Fi

Some convenience stores offer free Wi-Fi for customers.

💡 Free Wi-Fi can help tourists check maps or train routes quickly.

🖨 Copy Machines

Japanese convenience stores often have multi-function copy machines.

💡 You can print tickets, copy documents, and sometimes scan files.

📦 Delivery Service

Some convenience stores can help you send luggage or packages.

💡 This is useful for tourists traveling between cities with large suitcases.

🔞 Age Verification

When buying alcohol or cigarettes, you may need to press a confirmation button at the cashier.

💡 This is normal in Japan and does not mean you are in trouble.

🌙 Open 24 Hours

Many Japanese convenience stores are open 24 hours a day.

💡 Convenience stores are useful late at night when restaurants are closed.

🍱 Microwave Heating

Many meals in Japanese convenience stores are designed to be heated before eating.

💡 Staff usually heat the food for you at the cashier.
